THE 'RONA IS HERE.

COVID-19 is a big ass storm and it's made of shit.
For the past couple weeks, there has been a lot on my mind.

How did this happen?

Are people still hiring?

Is graduation happening on Zoom now?

How long is this gonna last?

When will I see
my friends again?

Are things improving?

What's gonna happen next?

It's been difficult to find my groove, my jive,
and my optimistic glass-half-full attitude.

What was supposed to be
my fresh first step into adulthood is paused,
and there's no definitive start in sight.

All I know is
my start is going to be different.

Everything from

greetings,

travelling,

shopping,

and even

my identity as an Asian American

is going to look and
feel different.

Every time I figure one thing out

something else comes up and

my future is still unclear.

I've been working on creating a space for myself.
The amount of relief and luck I feel about having a stable space to self-quarentine is a blessing, but it hasn't been easy by any means.

I feel claustrophobic. The walls are thin and the doors hide nothing. I feel like every thought I dare to speak out loud is amplified around this house and every little choice I make is on display for all to see. I can't even cook the food I want without someone coming and insisting on choking me of my adult independence and lurking and leering at what i'm doing. I can't even express that I want a glass of water without four different pairs of hands asking oh waht cup where like just leave me the fuck alone. Or I decide to cook something in the kitchen and all of a sudden she needs to be in the kitchen doing dishes or suddenly deciding to take inventory of the fridge. I have no space, no freedom, no privacy. The worst part is everyone is at their worst. Everyone feel stuck, everyone feels negative, and everyone feels hopeless and useless. The connections we depend on to fuel and sustain our existence are no longer viable and everyone is left to fend for themselves.

It might just be all in my head, but it's still real in my realm of existence.

I want to get control over my life.
I want to see my friends again,
go to the bookstore and browse for hours,
eat whatever food fits into my budget,
hang out with my partner in crowded Chinatown,
buy groceries and not stand in a weird line,
ride the subway without masks and gloves,

but I know that all of these things are going to be different.

what's gotten me out of this hole isnt panicking, applying to jobs, trying to fix my website, but the freedom of time i've allowed myself.

As stuff piles on I feel better.
